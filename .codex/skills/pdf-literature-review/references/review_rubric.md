# PDF Literature Review Rubric

Use this rubric after extracting PDF text.

## Classify each paper

- `direct`: directly supports AI publishing, AI-generated content governance, copyright, labeling, publishing ethics, or library handling of AI-generated materials
- `adjacent`: not about AI publishing itself, but useful for survey design, perception studies, ethics, or group-difference analysis
- `low`: weak fit for the current project

## Capture these fields

- bibliographic identity from first page
- research question
- sample and context
- variables or constructs
- statistical methods
- findings worth citing
- why it helps or does not help the current study

## Review priorities for this workspace

1. Can the paper justify survey constructs such as AI understanding, risk perception, or policy necessity?
2. Can it justify group comparisons such as library workers vs non-workers or high- vs low-use respondents?
3. Can it justify the information-treatment design before/after `E_pre` and `E_post`?
4. If the paper is only methodological, can it still support analysis choices?

## Red flags

- topic mismatch with no transferable method
- descriptive statistics only, with no construct or method relevance
- population too narrow to generalize without caution
- claims about law or policy with no primary source grounding
